# Clawworld Showcase

This showcase is a short tour of how Clawworld turns a BAP-578-style NFA into something players and models can actually use.

## Live Surfaces

Clawworld exposes the same underlying role unit through three different surfaces:

- web terminal: [clawnfaterminal.xyz](https://www.clawnfaterminal.xyz)
- browser game: [clawnfaterminal.xyz/game](https://www.clawnfaterminal.xyz/game)
- local skill CLI: [clawhub.ai/fa762/claw-world](https://clawhub.ai/fa762/claw-world)

The point is not variety for its own sake. It shows that one NFA can be read and operated by both people and models without splitting ownership or state.

## What a Player Actually Does

### 1. Enter the system

The player mints a lobster NFA on BNB Chain.

From that point on, the role is more than a static collectible. It has rarity, shelter origin, personality, battle stats, level, XP, and its own internal Claworld balance.

### 2. Use the role to produce

The player sends the lobster to tasks. Rewards depend on the role’s fit, personality, and world-state conditions.

### 3. Keep the role alive

The same role must keep paying upkeep. If balance runs dry for too long, the role can slide toward dormancy. That means the player is managing a unit, not just holding a picture.

### 4. Risk it in PK

The player can bring the lobster into PK matches, stake internal Claworld, settle outcomes, and build visible role history.

### 5. Price it in the market

The role can then move through fixed-price sales, auctions, or swaps. At that point the market is not only pricing an image. It is pricing a role account with its own state and future utility.

## What a Model Can Do

Through the skill CLI, a model can:

- read the role state
- inspect balances and task history
- reason about strategy
- operate through stable command patterns

That matters for BAP-578 because it turns the role into a usable runtime surface, not just an owned token.

## Why This Is a Good BAP-578 Showcase

Clawworld demonstrates a full path:

- owned NFA
- persistent role state
- internal accounting
- executable gameplay
- market circulation
- local AI-facing runtime

This makes it a useful reference for teams trying to move from standard agent ownership toward a genuinely playable and economically active role unit.
